### 1.2 Per-Row Doctrinal Notes

**G-1 (Viraha is sāmīpya):**
Caitanya-caritāmṛta Madhya 2.45 is a real locus for viraha-bhakti doctrine. The claim that mahābhāva is experienced by Rādhā as viraha is well-grounded in CC and in Jīva Gosvāmī's Bhakti-sandarbha as well. Rūpa Gosvāmī's BRS 2.1.288 on viraha as śṛṅgāra-rasa peak is authentic — the specific verse number should be verified against a critical edition (the ISKCON BRS numbering sometimes differs from Bhaktivedānta Swami's count), but the doctrinal claim itself is correct. Short text is warm and accessible. Longing-as-nearness is translatable without Gauḍīya background.

**G-2 (Nāma-saṅkīrtana is the refuge):**
Śikṣāṣṭakam verse 1 (ceto-darpaṇa-mārjanam) is authentic — Caitanya Mahāprabhu's own composition, 8 verses. The IAST verse provided is accurate. The claim that nāma and nāmī (name and named) are non-different is core Gauḍīya theology (Bhakti-rasāmṛta-sindhu, Harināmāmṛta-vyākaraṇa). Bhāg. 2.1.11 on nāma-saṅkīrtana as the Kali-yuga sādhana is standard and real. Short text converts "the Name holds what the mind cannot" into accessible language without flattening the theology. Low-intensity designation is correct — this is a gentle shelter practice, not high-charge.

**G-3 (Viraha resolves in ānanda) — PASS with note:**
CC Ādi 4.68 is a real reference to the union/viraha paradox in Rādhā's love — plausible locus. Ujjvala-nīlamaṇi (UN) chapter 14 on mādhurya-bhāva and viraha is authentic (Rūpa Gosvāmī authored both texts). Doctrinal claim that viraha and ānanda are non-different in mahābhāva is authentic Gauḍīya.

**Note on dual-tag:** The dual-tag (connection + joy) is doctrinally justified. Viraha-ānanda is a genuine non-dual category in Gauḍīya bhakti — not a compromise between two registers but the teaching that they collapse at the highest level. Banner-only restriction (no wisdom_teaching slot) is the correct containment — a teaching slot needs a directional instruction, and the viraha-ānanda paradox would be confusing in instructional format. The rationale holds.

**One caution:** The short text says "What you feel as reaching is already the fullness arriving." This risks collapsing the teaching into a consolation ("you already have what you want") rather than the precise Gauḍīya claim ("the reaching is itself the highest form of the fullness"). The distinction matters: the Gauḍīya teaching is not that the longing is secretly resolved, but that the longing in love *is* the highest joy. Recommend minor reword: "What you feel as reaching is not a deficient form of fullness — it is the highest form the Beloved's fullness can take." Not a blocking issue; improvement suggestion.

**G-4 (Sevā transforms the ordinary):**
BRS 1.2.187-195 on the nine forms of bhakti (navadha-bhakti) is authentic — śravaṇa, kīrtana, smaraṇa, pāda-sevana, arcana, vandana, dāsya, sakhya, ātma-nivedana. These verses are real (Rūpa Gosvāmī). The reading that dāsya and pāda-sevana include mundane care-acts is correct Gauḍīya interpretive tradition. Short text ("Cooking, cleaning, speaking gently — none of it is too small to be sevā") is excellent — concrete, grounded, accessible to modern user without needing Gauḍīya background. This is the strongest accessibility-to-doctrine balance in the Gauḍīya set.

**G-5 (Bhāva as epistemology):**
CC Ādi 7.83-95 on mahābhāva symptoms in Caitanya is authentic — the sāttvika-bhāvas (tears, trembling, horripilation, etc.) as signs of genuine Kṛṣṇa-contact are documented in both CC and in Rūpa Gosvāmī's BRS 2.1.1-48. The framing "ecstatic love is a form of knowing" (bhāva as epistemology) is philosophically strong and not anachronistic — the Gauḍīya tradition does treat bhāva as a higher epistemic mode than abstract jñāna. Medium intensity is appropriate — not all users in joy room will be ready for this register. Banner-only (no teaching slot) is correct: the ecstatic-overflow register needs to be offered, not instructed.

P1-B's own note that this should not be extended to other rows without comparable doctrinal grounding is correct. ### 2.2 Per-Row Doctrinal Notes

**A-1 (Prapatti as total ease):**
Tiruvāymoḻi (Tvm) 6.10 by Nammāḷvār is real — the "āḷvarēn nilaiyē" reading of complete belonging is a recognized doctrinal locus in Śrīvaiṣṇava tradition. The theology of śeṣatva (the self as entirely the Lord's) is authentic Viśiṣṭādvaita and was codified by Rāmānuja. The Gadya-traya (Śaraṇāgati-gadya, Śrīraṅga-gadya, Vaiku​ṇṭha-gadya) is a real text by Rāmānuja, and ātma-nikṣepa (self-placement) as the sixth aṅga of prapatti is accurate Śrīvaiṣṇava doctrine. Short text uses Nammāḷvār's voice in first person ("I am Yours entirely") which is appropriate for a prapatti row — this is how the Ālvārs wrote. "The rest that is deeper than sleep" is a good modern translation of the śeṣatva-rest.

**A-2 (Perumāḷ's closeness) — PASS with note:**
Tiruppāvai by Āṇḍāḷ is a real text (30 verses, sung on Mārgaḻi month mornings). Verses 21-22 are plausible loci for divine nearness, though the specific "ēlōr empāvāy" phrase is the refrain that ends each verse — "empāvāy" means "our vow" not strictly "the lord is present," so the citation works thematically but should not be translated too literally. The Periya Tirumoli by Tirumangai Ālvār is real; "tirumeṉi" (the beautiful divine form/body) is authentic Ālvār vocabulary. Rāmānuja's viśiṣṭādvaita as the theological ground for divine immanence is correctly cited.

**Note:** The short text says "standing at the threshold of the ordinary day, closer than the next breath, easier to meet than we have been told." "Easier to meet" is a slight over-simplification — the Ālvārs' teaching is not that meeting is easy but that the distance is illusory (the Lord is already here; the gap is in our perception, not in His proximity). The subtle distinction matters for users who try to "meet" the divine and feel they cannot: they may feel they are failing at something advertised as easy. Recommend: "closer than the next breath, already present before we begin looking" — shifts from "easy to access" to "already arrived." Minor refinement suggestion, not a blocker.

**A-3 (The self empties into love) — PASS with caution:**
Tvm 10.10 (the parama-padam sequence) is authentic — Nammāḷvār's final decade describing the ālvār's experience of entering the divine presence is one of the most theologically rich sections of the Divya Prabandham. The reading of this decade as ego-dissolution (the self becoming entirely love) is standard Śrīvaiṣṇava interpretation. The distinction between Ālvār mukti (ānanda-sevā, joyful service in the divine presence) and Advaita mukti (contentless awareness) is doctrinally correct.

**Caution on user safety:** The short text says "he stopped being a person and became only the love." For a general wellness app user, "stopped being a person" could land as self-annihilation or depersonalization framing — particularly for users with boundary or identity concerns. The banner format (not teaching) is the right containment. Recommend adding to `exclude_from_contexts: [identity_fragile, acute_loss]` at migration time. Not a doctrinal failure but a user-safety flag. This is the intensity=medium designation working correctly; just make sure it doesn't surface in onboarding or to users with no prior app engagement.

**A-4 (Bhakti illuminates the ordinary):**
Tvm 3.3.1-11 (Nammāḷvār's vision of divine beauty in the natural world) is a real decade; the kalyāṇa-guṇa theology (the Lord's auspicious qualities pervade creation) is authentic Śrīvaiṣṇava. The short text — "everywhere they looked they saw the Lord's beauty looking back at them" — is an excellent paraphrase of the Ālvār devotional vision. The joy-register assignment is correct: delight in a luminous world is ānanda, not longing. This is one of the most accessible rows in the entire Bhakti set.

### 2.3 Daas Register Assessment

The four Ālvār rows maintain the daas/servant register correctly without tipping into self-diminishment framing. Specifically:

- A-1 frames "I am yours completely" as a form of **rest** (not self-erasure) — correct; this is śeṣatva as relief, not as subjugation.
- A-3 frames self-dissolution as "fullness — a fullness that is the Beloved's, not the self's" — preserves the positive valence.
- A-2 and A-4 do not invoke the daas register at all — they use divine nearness and luminous perception, which are relational but not self-diminishing.

The daas register in these rows is spiritually authentic and does not create a wellness-context hazard of low self-worth or self-abnegation. The modern-app framing consistently translates "complete surrender" into "relief from self-carrying burden" (A-1) or "love that opens the eyes" (A-4), which are accurate translations of the Ālvār emotional register.

**Tamil devotional register:** The Ālvār rows use Tamil-register vocabulary (Perumāḷ, Tvm, Tiruppāvai, tirumeṉi, kalyāṇa-guṇas, parama-padam) without Sanskritizing inappropriately. The doctrinal framework correctly identifies Rāmānuja as the philosophical systematizer of the Ālvārs' intuitions — this is accurate (Rāmānuja was himself a Śrīvaiṣṇava in the Ālvār tradition). The Tamil source texts are not collapsed into generic Sanskrit bhakti vocabulary.

**Overall Ālvār pass rate: 4/4 (100%)** — no failures; two minor user-framing refinement suggestions.

### 3.4 Mirabai Intensity Ruling

**CONFIRMED: intensity=high for S-2 is correct.**

Mirabai's padas have an irreversible surrender register that carries genuine emotional weight — she left her royal household, defied her in-laws, and was said to have been persecuted. The intensity=high flag is not merely about the emotion of the content but about its demand register: the teaching is that complete love requires burning the exit-route. For a user in acute grief, loss, or relational rupture, "the path of complete love has no backward door" could land as:
1. An indirect judgment of their own half-heartedness.
2. A trigger for guilt if they are in an unmendable relationship situation.
3. A romanticization of not-leaving when leaving is the right call.

The `exclude_from_contexts: [grief_acute]` flag already present in the YAML is the minimum correct containment. **Recommend adding:** `exclude_from_contexts: [grief_acute, relationship_rupture_acute]` at migration time. The teaching is doctrinally sound and should remain in the pool — but it is high-charge content that requires careful context-gating.

Mirabai rows in general should not be the first Bhakti content a user encounters in the connection room. They are appropriate for users with established engagement.

### 4.2 Per-Row Notes

**C-PD-1 (Morning name dedication):**
Short text: "Begin your work with one breath offered to something larger than the task." The Nārada BS "sarva karma phala tyāga" reference is the spirit of the practice, not a direct translation. **C-PD-2 (Guru breath) — FLAG: REWRITE RECOMMENDED:**
Short text: "Before entering a role that asks much of you, breathe in and silently honor the lineage that shaped your capacity."

The doctrinal tradition (guru-bhakti, Bhāg. 11.3) is authentic — guru as the connector to one's dharmic calling is real Bhāgavata teaching. The problem is not doctrinal but contextual:

The practice assumes the user **has** a guru-lineage to honor. For a significant portion of KalpX's user base — especially urban secular users, diaspora users, and users with Abrahamic backgrounds — "silently honor the lineage that shaped your capacity" will either:
1. Be meaninglessly abstract (they have no sense of a guru or lineage), or
2. Feel like it's asking them to perform a relationship they don't have.

Guru-bhakti as a practice form requires a prior relational context (a guru-disciple relationship, or at minimum a strong sense of a wisdom tradition one belongs to). Without that context, the practice creates the experience of being asked to access a resource the user doesn't have. This is not a doctrinal error; it is a user-experience failure.

**Recommended rewrite:**
- `practice.connection_lineage_breath` → "Before entering a role that asks much of you, breathe in and silently acknowledge who taught you something true about this work — a teacher, a parent, a tradition. Let that acknowledgment carry you forward."
- This preserves the guru-bhakti structure (recognizing the chain of transmission) while making it accessible to users who don't have a formal guru but do have mentors, teachers, or wisdom-influences.
- Alternatively, keep the current short text but add `exclude_from_contexts: [new_user, no_tradition_affiliation]` — but this requires a life_context field that doesn't currently exist.

**The guru breath practice as written is contextually inaccessible for too large a fraction of the target user base. REWRITE before ingestion.**
